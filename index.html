Mahendra Singh Dhoni (Captaincy-2007 to 2016)

Captain Cool


MS Dhoni, the enigmatic cricketer from India, isobject-right-bottom not just a name but an embodiment of cricketing excellence and leadership. With his unorthodox style and calm demeanor, Dhoni redefined the role of a wicketkeeper-batsman, becoming a legend in his own right. His steely resolve under pressure earned him the moniker "Captain Cool," leading India to numerous memorable victories, including the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup triumph. Beyond his on-field heroics, Dhoni's humility and sportsmanship continue to inspire millions around the globe, leaving an indelible mark on the sport he loves.



Achievements

  • 2007 ICC World Twenty20: Under Dhoni's captaincy, India clinched the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 title in South Africa, defeating Pakistan in a thrilling final
  • 2010 and 2016 Asia Cup: Dhoni led India to victory in the Asia Cup twice during his captaincy tenure, in 2010 and 2016
  • 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup: Dhoni captained the Indian cricket team to glory in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, India's second World Cup victory, famously hitting the winning six in the final against Sri Lanka at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai
  • 2013 ICC Champions Trophy: Dhoni guided India to victory in the ICC Champions Trophy held in England in 2013, defeating hosts England in the final
  • Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in IPL: Dhoni captained the Chennai Super Kings to victory in the Indian Premier League (IPL) 5 times
